Appropriate Prepositions Meaning Example
Accused of Blamed for a wrongdoing He was accused of stealing the money. It's terrible to be accused of a crime you didn't commit.
Acquit of Declared not guilty The court acquitted him of all charges. The evidence was strong enough to acquit her of the crime.
Admit of Allow the possibility or truth of The situation admits of no easy solution. His success admits of no doubt.
Afraid of Fearful or scared of She's afraid of heights. I'm afraid of spiders.
Ashamed of Feeling shame or embarrassment He was ashamed of his behavior. She was ashamed of her mistake.
Assure of/Sure of Confident about I am sure of my decision. He assured me of his support.
Avail of Make use of or take advantage of Please avail of the opportunity to learn. They availed of the discount during the sale.
Aware of Conscious or informed about Are you aware of the new policy? She is aware of the risks involved.
Blind to Lacking awareness or discernment He is blind to the consequences. She seems blind to the suffering of others.
Capable of Having the ability or capacity She is capable of solving complex problems. Are you capable of handling this responsibility?
Consist of Composed or made up of The team consists of experienced professionals. The cake consists of layers of chocolate and cream.
Cure of Medical treatment to relieve or heal There is no cure for the common cold. He is seeking a cure for his chronic condition.
Deprive of Take away or deny The loss deprived her of happiness. They were deprived of basic necessities.
Destitute of Lacking or without The village was destitute of basic amenities. His speech was destitute of emotion.
Devoid of Completely lacking or empty of His explanation was devoid of logic. The room was devoid of any furniture.
Die of Cause of death He died of a heart attack. Many people in the region die of starvation.
Fond of Having a liking for She is fond of playing the piano. He is fond of adventure sports.
Get rid of Eliminate or discard Let's get rid of the old furniture. She decided to get rid of toxic relationships.
Guilty of Responsible for a crime He was found guilty of theft. She was guilty of breaking the rules.
Ignorant of Lacking knowledge about She is ignorant of the facts. Many people are ignorant of the consequences of their actions.
Inform of Provide information about Please inform me of any changes. The manager informed the team of the new project.
Proud of/Boast of Feeling pride or satisfaction She is proud of her achievements. He boasted of his success to everyone.
Take care of Attend to or look after I will take care of the plants while you're away. Can you take care of my cat this weekend?
Think of Consider or contemplate What do you think of this idea? I often think of my childhood memories.
Victim Of One who suffers from He is a victim of cyberbullying. She became a victim of identity theft.
Worthy of Deserving or having merit His actions are worthy of admiration. The book is worthy of reading.
Acceptable to Agreeable or satisfactory to Is this plan acceptable to you? The proposal was not acceptable to the majority.
Access to Availability or entry to The students have access to the library. Employees have limited access to confidential information.
Accountable to Responsible or answerable to Employees are accountable to their managers. The CEO is accountable to the board of directors.
Accustomed to Familiar or used to She is accustomed to the cold weather. I am not accustomed to public speaking.

Addicted to Dependent on or obsessed with He is addicted to video games. She became addicted to caffeine.
Adhere to Stick to or follow closely It's essential to adhere to the rules. She adheres to a strict daily routine.
Admit to Confess or acknowledge He finally admitted to his mistake. She admitted to breaking the vase.
Agree to Consent or approve I agree to your proposal. They agreed to the terms of the contract.
Alternative to Option or substitute for Walking is a good alternative to driving. Can you suggest an alternative to this solution?
Answer to Respond to or be accountable to The manager answers to the company's CEO. We all have to answer to someone in our jobs.
Attentive to Paying attention or considerate of Be attentive to the details. He is always attentive to his wife's needs.
Belong to Be the property or member of This book belongs to the library. The house belongs to my grandparents.
Callous to Insensitive or unfeeling towards He is callous to others' feelings. Her comments were callous to the pain he was experiencing.
Cling to Hold on tightly or be emotionally attached She clings to her childhood memories. In difficult times, people often cling to familiar things.
Confess to Admit or acknowledge guilt He decided to confess to the crime. She confessed to her mistake immediately.
Confined to Limited to or restricted within The discussion is confined to this room. The damage was confined to a specific area.
Contrast to Difference or opposition to This is in stark contrast to what he said earlier. Her opinion is in contrast to mine.
Contribute to Contribute or be a factor in Regular exercise can contribute to good health. Your efforts contribute to the success of the project.
Detrimental to Harmful or damaging to Smoking is detrimental to your health. Lack of sleep can be detrimental to your performance.
Devoted to Committed or dedicated to She is devoted to her career. He is devoted to his family.
Engaged to a person Formally agreed to marry someone She is engaged to her longtime boyfriend. They got engaged to each other last month.
Exposed to Subjected to or unprotected from The baby should not be exposed to direct sunlight. He was exposed to different cultures during his travels.
Glad to Happy or pleased to I am glad to hear about your success. She was glad to help with the project.
Grateful to Thankful towards I am grateful to you for your help. She's grateful to her parents for their support.
Harmful to Causes harm or damage to Smoking is harmful to your health. Using this product may be harmful to the environment.
Heir to Inherits or succeeds He is the heir to a vast fortune. She is the heir to the family business.
In order to For the purpose of We work hard in order to achieve our goals. He studied in order to pass the exam.

Introduce to Present or bring into contact with Allow me to introduce you to my colleague. She introduced me to classical music.
Junior to Lower in rank or position He is junior to me in the company hierarchy. She is junior to him in school.

Look forward to Anticipate or await eagerly I look forward to meeting you. She looks forward to the weekends.

Sincere to Genuine or truthful towards She is sincere to her friends. Be sincere to yourself and others.

True to Faithful or consistent with He remained true to his principles. Stay true to your values.
Hankered after Strongly desired or yearned for He hankered after success from a young age. She hankered after adventure.
